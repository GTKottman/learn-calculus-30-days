# Checkpoint schedule (Calculus I)

Checkpoints occur on **Days 7, 14, 21, and 28**. Day **30** is the comprehensive capstone (not a timed “exam” block in static Markdown—use it as your final rehearsal).

| Checkpoint | Day | Scope (high level) |
|------------|-----|---------------------|
| **Checkpoint 1** | 7 | Average and instantaneous rates; difference quotient; limit definition of derivative; limits (two-sided and one-sided), limit laws; continuity; types of discontinuity; IVT (statement + hypotheses). |
| **Checkpoint 2** | 14 | Derivative rules (sums, constant multiple, product, quotient where needed); trig derivatives; squeeze theorem (conceptual); chain rule; exponentials and logarithms; graphing with \(f'\) and \(f''\); asymptotes and limits at \(\pm\infty\); implicit differentiation. |
| **Checkpoint 3** | 21 | Closed-interval optimization; MVT (consequences + solving for \(c\)); related rates setup; L’Hôpital (when it applies); Taylor polynomials (coefficients, center, low-degree patterns). |
| **Checkpoint 4** | 28 | Riemann sums; FTC I and II; substitution and integration by parts setup; area between curves; volume (disk/washer/shell at syllabus depth); arc length and surface of revolution (setup); average value of a function; improper integrals (basic convergence splits and \(p\)-type intuition). |

**How to use:** Attempt checkpoints **closed-book** first, then check solutions. Revisit `controllers/srs-queue.md` for spaced prompts tied to weak areas.
